PERSONAL PROJECT — IP
PRE-FILING · CONFIDENTIAL · INDIA + US
When Google Maps stops — this picks up.

Google Maps gets a delivery partner to your building gate. Past that — through the internal lanes, past the parking lot, up the right lift, to the actual door — it's completely blind. So every delivery partner calls the resident. Every time. For every delivery.

I built a platform that eliminates that call. Residents draw their own path from gate to door once — with their finger on a map. Every delivery partner, guest, or visitor who arrives at the gate follows it live on their phone. No call. No confusion. No repeat.

The product introduces concepts that don't exist anywhere in the prior art: a portable location identity that any platform can call via open API, AR guidance through private spaces recorded by the resident once, and time-conditioned routing that switches entry points automatically at dispatch.

13 patent claims across 3 applications. India provisional first, US provisional strategy in parallel. Nothing disclosed publicly until filing is complete — which is why this description stays vague.

APPLICATION 01 — LOCATION IDENTITY
@username that navigates you to someone's door
A portable user handle that resolves — across any platform, via open API, without a bilateral deal — to a complete navigation profile including route, time rules, and drop-off preferences. Zomato and Amazon can both call it. They get different gates.
APPLICATION 02 — NAVIGATION EXPERIENCE
AR from gate to door, through spaces no map knows
Resident records a walkthrough once. Delivery partner's camera shows AR arrows guiding them exactly where the resident walked — through private buildings, unmapped compounds, to the actual door. Prior art covers public outdoor spaces. This covers everywhere else.
APPLICATION 03 — PLATFORM & PRIVACY
Coordinates that never leave the server
The delivery partner's phone receives "turn left in 20m" — never raw GPS coordinates. Your home's location stays on the server. DPDP Act compliant by architecture, not by policy. The asymmetry (resident draws, partner follows, neither can switch roles) is enforced at API layer.
